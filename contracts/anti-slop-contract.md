# Anti-Slop Contract

Things that look like they belong on a generic 2024–2026 landing page —
creator-economy, SaaS, crypto, AI-startup. We do not ship them. If you reach for
one, the brief is being executed wrong.

The **positive reference** is the hero in `index.html`: a fixed Tron stage, one
centred mark, drawn in hairlines. When in doubt, do what the hero does — not what
a template does. (`anti-ai-contract.md` covers *why* these read as machine-made;
this contract is the concrete list.)

This contract is enforceable. One item present is one fail.

## Layout slop

- **Bento grids.**
- **Three-up feature cards** — an icon, a title, one sentence, ×3 in a row.
- **Alternating dark/light section bands.** There is one ground; it never
  changes (`style-contract.md` §2).
- The **centred-headline + centred-subhead + two-centred-buttons** hero
  (primary + ghost button).
- **Floating glass panels**, frosted-blur bars, neumorphic surfaces.
- **Boxed cards** — closed rectangles drawn around content. The Tron language is
  open brackets and hairlines, not boxes (`style-contract.md` §4).
- **Logo walls** — "Trusted by," rows of greyed-out company logos.
- **Testimonial carousels.**
- **"How it works"** numbered 1 / 2 / 3 in big circles.
- **FAQ accordions** used as page filler.
- A **pricing table, leaderboard, or dashboard screenshot** on the landing page.

## Motion slop

- **Headline characters that fade in one by one** as a flourish.
- **Number counters that tick up on scroll** for no real reason.
- **"Scanning" line effects** sweeping across hero text.
- **Cards that tilt / parallax toward the cursor** on hover.
- **Confetti or sparkle bursts** on a button hover or click.
- **Drifting or looping background gradients.** The shader is the one carved
  exception — and it earns it by reacting to the cursor (`anti-ai-contract.md`),
  not by drifting decoratively.
- **Parallax for parallax's sake** — layers moving at different speeds with no
  meaning.

## Copy slop

When real copy is added — so far just the `addictd.ai` wordmark — it refuses:

- **Hype words** — "supercharge," "unleash," "revolutionary," "next-gen,"
  "game-changing," "10x," "AI-powered," "seamless," "effortless," "cutting-edge,"
  "world-class."
- **Exclamation marks. Emoji. ✨.**
- **"We believe," "imagine a world where."**
- **Fake scarcity** — countdown timers, "only N spots left," FOMO badges.
- Headline shapes to refuse outright: "The future of X is here," "X,
  reimagined," "Unleash your X," "Built for the next generation of X."

The brand name is lowercase **addictd**, the site **addictd.ai**. (The hero's HUD
text — `SYS · ADDICTD // INIT 0.0.1` — is deliberately uppercased system-chrome
in the mono register; it is not the wordmark and not prose.)

## Interaction slop

- A **cookie banner** that scrolls with the page.
- A **chat-widget bubble** in the corner.
- A **newsletter modal** that pops after N seconds.
- A **"Subscribe to our blog"** footer.
- A **blinking "Now accepting…" urgency badge.**

## Why this exists

Every item above is what this brief becomes on autopilot — and they are
interchangeable across a thousand sites. addictd's design is recognisable only by
what it refuses. The contract is not about taste; it is about not dissolving into
a category of indistinguishable dark-purple landing pages.

When in doubt: **subtract.**
